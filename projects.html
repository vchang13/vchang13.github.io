Personal Projects

Glass Bottle A project for Google's Computer Science Summer Institute inspired by our group's stories of stress and anxiety in high school. After sharing stories of sleepless nights, testing anxiety, and the competitive nature of school, we decided to create a tool that allows people to vent about their problems without feeling like they're bothering others. This simple web app allows for users to vent their feelings and then symbolically send them off. If users would like to share their thoughts, an anonymous posts page saves them for all to read. My contributions to this project involves creating the glass bottle, posting, and sharing features as well as collaborating with other group members to implement Google login capabilities and the design of the site.

DotBook Task Organizer A project for Girls Who Code's Summer Immersion Program inspired by our group's collective need for organization and love for stickers. Taking inspiration from bullet journalling, we created this rudimentary web app that could organize tasks and notes in a clean and simple manner while also allowing for personalization and fun. My contributions to this project involves creating the task making and note creation features and collaborating with other group members in creating stickers and their functionality.

$aveur Created in twelve hours for the University of Texas at Austin's Women in Computer Science Hackathon (WiCS Hacks). Inspired by our frustrations with budgeting and inputing transactions by hand, we wanted to create a tool that could track transactions automatically as well as keep users accountable for their purchases. Using Capital One's Nessie API and the Google Maps API, we created a web app that uses your location and transactions to track, record, and limit your spending. My contributions to this project involves implementing the Google Maps API.
